Closing Remarks

We close this chapter with an observation of what has not worked and a redisplay of one of the most useful tools in the book: the value of the IX graph.

What Has Not Worked

The most common failure mode for an IXP is the “Field of Dreams” approach â€“ “build it and they will come.” Eastern Africa for example is filled with ethernet switches intended to be the base IXPs for the countries. They have few or no members and no one is promoting the IXP. This path is guaranteed to fail. The “Field of Dreams” approach is almost as bad as having an IXP operated by the incumbent ISP or required by the telecommunications regulator. As shown earlier, there is a long path to build the population up to critical mass where it is valuable to the peering population.
